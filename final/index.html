Shrinking Population of Japan

By Ayako Watanabe

Introduction

According to the National Institute of Population and Social Security Research, 15% of the population of Japan is projected to decrease significantly and the number of people above 60 years old will be 43% by 2045. The main reasons are the declining fertility rate and closed policy for immigrants.

In an effort to address this issue, we visualize the trends of demographic shift and examine why this projection is reasonable. Scroll down to learn more.
